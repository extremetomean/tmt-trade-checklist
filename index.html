01 — The Scalp LONG ONLY3M ONLY

Price enters the Scalp Zone and looks for a quick reversion back toward the 3M TMA.

Step 1 — Trigger
Price enters the Scalp Zone between the two Scalp labels.
The Scalp Zone itself is the trigger. This setup is long only and 3M only.
3M TMA is the reversion target.
This is not a trend trade. The goal is a fast move back toward mean.
Step 2 — Entry Rules
Take the contract when price enters the Scalp Zone.
Do not wait for TMA confirmation. Price inside the zone is the entry trigger.
Optional add only if the candle closes inside the Scalp Zone.
The add is optional. The core trade is the first contract taken inside the zone.
Step 3 — Target
Look for a quick reversion back toward the 3M TMA.
This is a fast mean-reversion trade, not a hold-and-hope trade.
Full exit when price reaches the 3M TMA.
The 3M TMA is the target. Once price gets there, the scalp is complete.
Step 4 — Stop
STOP: Exit the full trade if price closes below the Scalp Zone STOP
A close below the zone means the scalp failed. No averaging down and no second guessing.
Key principle: This is a simple, fast reversion trade. Price enters the Scalp Zone, we take the contract, and we look for a quick move back toward the 3M TMA. The zone is the setup; no extra confirmation is required.

02 — Extreme to Mean LONG ONLY

Price reaches a downside Extreme level and reverts back to the moving average that generated that Extreme.

Step 1 — Trigger Levels
Price reaches the downside 15M MMA Extreme.
Full target is the 15M MMA.
Price reaches the downside Hourly TMA Extreme.
Full target is the Hourly TMA.
Price reaches the downside Hourly MMA Extreme.
Full target is the Hourly MMA.
Step 2 — Entry Rules
Contract 1 — taken the moment price first touches or reaches the Extreme level.
The downside Extreme touch is the first valid long entry.
Contract 2 — optional add if a candle closes at or beyond the Extreme level.
Add only after the candle close confirms the extension.
Step 3 — Target
Partial exit at the 15M TMA when time constraints apply.
Mandatory when no overnight hold or session-end risk applies.
Full exit at the moving average that generated the Extreme.
15M MMA Extreme → 15M MMA. H TMA Extreme → H TMA. H MMA Extreme → H MMA.
If holding toward the full target is not possible due to session end, exit remaining position at close.
Do not turn a mechanical reversion trade into overnight exposure.
Step 4 — Stop
STOP: Stop is placed beyond the Extreme level that triggered the entry STOP
The trade idea fails beyond the Extreme level.
STOP: If price makes a new extreme beyond the entry signal level, exit immediately STOP
Do not average in after the level fails.
Key principle: The target moving average always matches the Extreme level that triggered the trade. This setup is long only, so only downside Extreme levels are valid.

03 — Reversion to Mean LONG ONLY

The 3M TMA curls upward toward a separated TRMA — fade the TMA back to the TRMA.

Step 1 — Required Conditions
Space exists between the 3M TMA and TRMA.
They must be separated, not compressed or touching.
TRMA direction is irrelevant.
It can be bullish, bearish, or flat. The space and TMA curl matter.
The 3M TMA must visibly curl upward toward the TRMA.
Long only. The TRMA must be above the TMA.
Step 2 — Entry Rules
Enter on the 3M TMA curl.
The entry candle is the candle where the TMA changes slope upward toward the TRMA.
Confirm the TMA is curling toward a TRMA that is above it.
No short-side version for this setup.
Step 3 — Target
Full exit when price reaches the TRMA.
The reversion is complete at the TRMA.
Partial exit allowed at key OB or support/resistance levels inside the structure.
Partial is allowed, but the full mechanical target remains the TRMA.
Step 4 — Stop
STOP: Exit if the 3M TMA curls back down before reaching the TRMA STOP
The trade is off when the TMA reverses away from the target.
STOP: No discretionary override after the TMA reverses STOP
If TMA reverses, the setup is invalidated.
Key principle: This is the primary bread-and-butter setup. Trades 01 and 02 revert price to the TMA. This trade reverts the TMA to the TRMA — one structural level higher. Space between the MAs is the non-negotiable prerequisite.

04 — The Trend LONG OR SHORT

TRMA changes direction — ride the new trend until it reverses.

Step 1 — Trigger
The TRMA changes slope direction from bearish to bullish.
The thick blue MA flips up. That flip is the trade signal.
This is the only condition required.
No additional filters. The TRMA flip is the complete signal.
Step 2 — Entry Rules
Enter long on the candle where the TRMA bullish direction change is confirmed.
The flip candle is the entry candle.
No additional filters — the TRMA slope change is the complete signal.
Do not require TMA, candles, or discretionary confirmation.
Step 3 — Target
No fixed price target.
Hold for the full duration of the bullish TRMA trend.
The next TRMA reversal is the only exit trigger.
Let it run until the TRMA flips back down.
Step 4 — Stop
STOP: Exit immediately when the TRMA reverses bearish STOP
The TRMA is both the entry signal and the exit signal.

05 — The Continuation LONG OR SHORT

After a trend is established, the TMA pulls back to the TRMA and re-curls — enter the next leg.

Step 1 — Required Conditions
A prior bullish trend must already be established.
TRMA is directional from Trade 04 context.
TMA pulls back toward the TRMA and the two MAs compress.
TMA does not cross through the TRMA.
TMA remains above the TRMA.
For a long continuation, TMA must stay on the correct side.
Step 2 — Entry Rules
Enter when the TMA curls back up in the trend direction after compression.
The re-curl after the pullback is the entry signal.
Do not enter on the pullback itself.
Wait for the TMA re-curl to confirm the next leg.
Step 3 — Target
First target: prior swing high.
This is the level the original move failed at or paused near.
Second target: TMA Extreme if continuation carries through the prior swing.
Only target the Extreme if price continues beyond the prior swing.
Step 4 — Stop
STOP: Exit if the TMA crosses back down through the TRMA STOP
The continuation is invalidated.
STOP: If price returns into consolidation and TMA re-crosses TRMA, the setup is off STOP
Do not keep forcing the continuation.
